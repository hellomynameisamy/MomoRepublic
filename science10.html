    Atoms and Molecules

an atom is the smallest particle of an element that retains the propertues of that element. 'most' atoms consists of: protons, neutrons, and electrons

2 or more atoms can chemically combine in specific ways by sharing 1 or more pairs of electrons to form molecules. A molecule is the smallest particle of a COVALENT COMPOUND that retains the properties. exampe: H2O

Chemical Change

Involves breaking chemical bonds between atoms of pure substances then rearranging the atoms and reforming bonds in new ways.

    a chemical equation

  • is what happens when one or more chemical change occurs at the same given time
  • Obeys the LOCOM aka the law of conservation of mass
  • The reactants change into new substances with different properties, these are called products

    • Chemical Equations can be written in different ways

  • A word equation: can be written in many different ways...
  • example: nitrogen monoxide + oxygen ---> nitrogen dioxide
  • a symbolic equation
  • example: 2NO(g) + 02(g) + 2NO2(g)

    • Evidence of chemical change...

  • precipitate formed
  • gas produced
  • heat produced
  • light produced
  • color change
  • change in smell
  • energy consumed/energy given off (temperature change
  • the change is irreversible most of the time

    • Law of Conservation of Mass aka LOCOM

  • chemical change means that new compounds are created
  • BUT... atoms are neither created nor destroyed
  • Dalton realized that atoms simply rearrange themselves during chemical reactions
  • Total number of atoms of an element among reactants is equal to the atoms of that element among the products
  • This is why chemical equations must be balanced

    • LOCOM (Lavoirsier, 18th c.

  • in a chemical reaction, the total mass of the products equals the total mass of the reactants; mass is conserved

    • Writing and Balancing Chemical Equations

  • a word equation just shows the element/compounds invovlved.
  • a balanced chemical equation shows all the atoms in their correct quantities
  • make sure that the number of each atom is the same on both sides of the reaction arrow
  • always use the smallest whole number ratio

    • Hints for Writing Word Equations

  • Be careful of molecules of diatomic and polyatomic elements, such as O2, P4, and S8
  • The "special seven" are all diatomic elements- when they occur on their own, they are written as..
  • use the chemical symbol for elements when they are not in a compound (ie. Cu represents pure / solid copper)
  • Several common covalent molecules containing H have common names that do not help in writing chemical formulae

    • Strategies for Balancing Chemical Equations

  • Do not change subscripts
  • balance compounds first, elements last
  • if hydrogen and oxygen occur in several places, balance them last
  • you can sometimes balance polyatomic ions as a group, if they exist of both sides of the reaction arrow
  • last but not least, always double check once you think you are finished
  • Always reduce
  • After balancing the diatomics, if the remaining elements are not equal for example 7 or like an odd number in general, just double it
