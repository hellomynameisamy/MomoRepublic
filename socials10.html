How a bill is passed

The First Reading

  • the bill is printed and distrubuted
  • no debate;vote is automatic

    • The Second Reading

  • The contents within the bill starts to get debated about.
  • This is the most significant debate stage
  • The debate is about the principal behind it. "What is the purpose of this law?
  • Second vote is taken. If passed it goes on to the committee stage.

    • Committee Stage

  • Select groups of MPs and experts within the area of the bill study the bill clause by clause.
  • Specific legal language gets sorted out.
  • Amendments are proposed.
  • The committee then proposes their findings to the House.

    • Third Reading

  • The House briefly discusses what the committee came up with.
  • Then the 3rd vote is held.
  • If it is approved by the House of Commons through vote it will be sent to the Senate for review and approval.
  • If it fails it gets sent back to the committee.

    • To the Senate

  • First Reading: Bill is distributed automatic vote.
  • Second Reading: Bill is debated, discussed and voted.
  • Sent to Committee Stage.
  • Third Reading: Bill is debated once more.
  • If approved it goes to the royal assent.

    • Royal Assent

  • On behalf of the Queen, the Governor General signs the bill.
  • Bill is now a statute law-enforceable by law and there are consequences if not followed.
    • Provincial/Territorial Law
  • Laws made in the provincial government that are passed are made in a very similiar process. Except they do not have a Senate.
  • Each province has a Lieutenant General;representatives of the queen
