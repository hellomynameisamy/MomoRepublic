        Unit 1 - Learning Expectations (By the end of this unit you should be able toâ€¦.)

        Origin of life
      1. State the 7 characteristics that living things share.
      2. Explain the Oparin-Haldane Hypothesis. What they believed the conditions on earth were at the time life originated and the three steps they hypothesized to have led to the first living organism. Explain the Miller-Urey experiment. What were they testing? How did they set up their experiment? What did their experiment prove?

        3. Classification
      3. State that the binomial system of names for species is universal among biologists and has been agreed and developed at a series of congresses.
      4. Outline the benefits of the binomial system of naming species.
      5. State that taxonomists classify species using a hierarchy of taxa and when species are discovered they are given scientific names using the binomial system.
      6. State the 7 taxon developed by Carl Linneaus
      7. Outline the three domains that all organisms are classified into.
      8. Construct a dichotomous keys for use in identifying specimens.
      9. Explain what homologous traits are and explain how they are related to inaccurate classification of species.
      10. Outline what a cladogram is and what it illustrates.

        11. Construct a cladogram.
      11. Analyze a cladogram to deduce the evolutionary relationships between related species.
      12. Explain the domain system of classification
      13. Outline the difference between the domains

        14. Prokaryotes vs. Eukaryotes
      14. Compare and contrast prokaryote and eukaryote cells with regard to their structures and the functions of their structures.
      15. Draw and/or label a diagram of a prokaryotic cell, including the cell wall, plasma membrane, cytoplasm, pili, flagella, ribosomes and nucleoid with naked DNA.
      16. Draw and/or label a diagram of a eukaryotic cell, including plasma membrane, cytoplasm, ribosomes, nucleus, mitochondria and other membrane-bound organelles.

        17. Photosynthesis
      17. Define heterotroph and autotroph
      18. Outline the difference between a phototroph and a chemotroph
      19. Define photosynthesis. Outline the word and balanced chemical equation for photosynthesis.
      20. Outline the structure and function of the chloroplast.
      21. Outline the role and location of chlorophyll
      22. Outline the wavelengths of visible light that chlorophyll most effectively absorbs and reflects.
      23. Outline the structure and function of ATP.
      24. Draw an ATP and ADP molecule
      25. Outline how temperature, light intensity and carbon dioxide concentration are possible limiting factors on the rate of photosynthesis.
      26. Explain the graphs plotting the Effect of limiting factors on the rate of photosynthesis
      27. Outline the steps of the light dependent and light independent reactions of photosynthesis (the electron transport chain and the Calvin cycle) with respect to where in the cell each step occurs and what are the products of these reactions

        28. Cellular Respiration
      28. Define cell respiration.
      29. Outline anaerobic respiration.
      30. Outline lactate production in humans when anaerobic respiration is used to maximize the power of muscle contractions.
      31. Contrast anaerobic respiration with aerobic respiration.
      32. Outline the steps of cell respiration (glycolysis, pyruvate oxidation (link reaction), the Krebs cycle and the electron transport chain/chemiosmosis) What is the net ATP and NADP production of each step and where in the cell each step occurs.
      33. Outline the structure and function of the mitochondrion.
